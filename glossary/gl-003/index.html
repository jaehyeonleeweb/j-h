‚ãØ Designing programmes can also mean: inventing rules of arrangement. Taking a chemical reaction as a parallel, the designer must try to find a group of new combinations by reference to a kind of formula. The formula is paramount. The formula creates the form. Creates a group of forms. Thus, for instance, there is a formula in poetry corresponding to this conception. The traditional structure of language is dissolved. No grammar. No syntax. The elements are single words. They stand loose in the line with all their valencies free. The rule of the game is permutation. The poems arising are called constellations. Constellations are a poetic programme.1
